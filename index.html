Red Wine Quality Analysis

Physicochemical properties of vinho verde and perceived quality

This project investigates the relationships between measurable wine properties and quality ratings across 1,599 red vinho verde wines from Portugal. The analysis aims to answer two questions:

  1. Which physicochemical properties relate most strongly to quality — and which do not?
  2. What properties would you recommend testing next to increase the quality rating?

Dataset

Each wine is described by 11 physicochemical input variables (fixed acidity, volatile acidity, citric acid, residual sugar, chlorides, free sulfur dioxide, total sulfur dioxide, density, pH, sulphates, alcohol) and one output variable: quality, a subjective score from 0–10 calculated as the median rating of three taste testers.

Source: P. Cortez et al., Modeling wine preferences by data mining from physicochemical properties, Decision Support Systems, Elsevier, 47(4):547–553, 2009. Dataset available on Kaggle.
